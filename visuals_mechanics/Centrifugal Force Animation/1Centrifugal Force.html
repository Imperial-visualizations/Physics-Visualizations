The Centrifugal Force (given by \(-m\boldsymbol{\omega} \times (\boldsymbol{\omega} \times \mathbf{r}\))) is another of the inertial forces. This force always points out away from the Earth and so acts as a correction to gravity.

It does not cause any deviations in the plane of the Earth's surface, but is accounted for by a slightly changed value of g which changes the shape of the Earth to an oblate spheroid (squashed orange).
